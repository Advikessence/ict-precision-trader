ICT Strategy Guide

Overview

This strategy uses ICT (Inner Circle Trader) concepts based on liquidity, smart money manipulation, and time-based candle ranges to capture high-probability trade setups during the NY session.

Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Use the 4-Hour Timeframe
  • Go to the 4-hour chart.
  • Mark the High and Low of the 4-hour candle from 9:00 PM to 1:00 AM (New York time).
  • This candle range represents the liquidity zone for the session.
Step 2: Drop to the 3-Minute Timeframe
  • Once the range is marked, move to the 3-minute chart.
  • This smaller timeframe allows for precision entries.
Step 3: Wait for a Liquidity Sweep
  • Watch for price to sweep either the High or Low of the marked 4H candle.
  • This indicates a liquidity grab ‚Äî a common manipulation before the real move.
Step 4: Wait for CISD (Change in State of Delivery)
  • After the sweep, wait for a market structure shift (e.g., break of internal structure or BOS).
  • This confirms a potential reversal and indicates smart money is active.
Step 5: Enter the Trade
  • Look for an entry at one of the following zones:
  • FVG (Fair Value Gap)
  • BB (Breaker Block) Range
  • OB (Order Block)
  • These are institutional price levels where price often reacts.
Step 6: Set Stop Loss and Target
  • Place your SL (Stop Loss) according to Safe SL Theory just above/below the sweep or structure.
  • Target a 1:3 Risk-to-Reward Ratio (RRR) or more.

Pro Tips

  • Be patient; let the sweep and CISD play out before entering.
  • Always confirm with price action or liquidity reaction.
  • Use time-of-day confluence for best results (especially during NY Open or London-NY overlap).
  • Some pairs like EUR/USD and GBP/USD show stronger ICT patterns during specific sessions.
  • Gold (XAU/USD) often shows exaggerated sweeps before reversals.
