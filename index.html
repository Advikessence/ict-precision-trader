ICT Precision Trading Strategy

This strategy uses ICT (Inner Circle Trader) concepts based on liquidity, smart money manipulation, and time-based candle ranges to capture high-probability trade setups during the NY session.

Use the 4-Hour Timeframe

Go to the 4-hour chart and mark the High and Low of the 4-hour candle from 9:00 PM to 1:00 AM (New York time). This candle range represents the liquidity zone for the session.

Drop to the 3-Minute Timeframe

Once the range is marked, move to the 3-minute chart. This smaller timeframe allows for precision entries.

Wait for a Liquidity Sweep

Watch for price to sweep either the High or Low of the marked 4H candle. This indicates a liquidity grab â€” a common manipulation before the real move.

Wait for CISD (Change in State of Delivery)

After the sweep, wait for a market structure shift (e.g., break of internal structure or BOS). This confirms a potential reversal and indicates smart money is active.

Enter the Trade

Look for an entry at one of the following zones:

  • FVG (Fair Value Gap)
  • BB (Breaker Block) Range
  • OB (Order Block)

These are institutional price levels where price often reacts.

Set Stop Loss and Target

Place your SL (Stop Loss) according to Safe SL Theory just above/below the sweep or structure. Target a 1:3 Risk-to-Reward Ratio (RRR) or more.

Pro Tips

  • Be patient; let the sweep and CISD play out before entering.
  • Always confirm with price action or liquidity reaction.
  • Use time-of-day confluence for best results (especially during NY Open or London-NY overlap).
  • Some pairs like EUR/USD and GBP/USD show stronger ICT patterns during specific sessions.
  • Gold (XAU/USD) often shows exaggerated sweeps before reversals.
